## 🏛️ CRITICAL: IP Ownership — Do This First

### The Problem
Under New Zealand's **Copyright Act 1994**, works created "in the course of employment" belong to your employer — the school — not you. This is the default position.

**If you built the chemical database or digital signage system:**
- Using school computers → school may own it
- On school time (even partially) → school may own it
- Using school data (chemical inventory, SDS sheets) → school owns that data

### The TKI Model (Standard NZ Approach)
The Ministry of Education's TKI guidelines give schools three options:
- **Option A:** School retains copyright but licenses you to develop it further
- **Option B:** Teacher retains copyright, grants school a licence
- **Option C:** Negotiated case-by-case

### What To Do Right Now
1. **Have an informal conversation** with your principal before this becomes a formal issue
2. **Get written agreement** — even an email trail — that you own the IP or have licence to commercialise
3. **Frame it positively:** "I've been building tools that help our school. I'd like to offer these to other schools too. Can we work out an arrangement?"
4. **Consider offering your school a free perpetual licence** in exchange for IP ownership — most principals will say yes
5. **Get a NZ employment lawyer to review** your employment agreement (~$300 for a 1-hour consult, worth every cent)

### Going Forward
- Build new versions on your **own device, own time, own accounts**
- Keep detailed records of when you work on these projects (evenings, weekends)
- Consider a formal IP assignment agreement with the school

- Fits how schools budget (annual, not monthly)

- ~2,500 NZ schools (primary + secondary)

Your warm path is: **teacher contact → HOD/IT → principal**. Don't start at the top.

### What You Have

- Chemical inventory management
- Expiry date tracking and alerts
- Built for NZ school context

### Competitor Landscape

| Product | NZ School Price | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-------|
| **Chemwatch (via Crescendo NZ)** | **NZ$450–590/yr** | Official NZ school reseller; GoldFFX + Smarter Suite; 1 Mar–28 Feb subscription cycle |
| **ChemAlert (RMT Global)** | Enterprise pricing (estimate NZ$3,000–8,000/yr) | Primarily mining/manufacturing; overkill for schools |
| **cheminventory.net** | USD $99–299/yr depending on users | US-focused, not NZ SDS library |

**The real competitor:** Chemwatch via Crescendo at $450–590/yr is the baseline NZ schools currently pay. Many schools pay nothing and use paper-based systems (huge compliance risk).

### Your Differentiation
- Potentially significantly cheaper than Chemwatch
- NZ-specific: built around NZ H&S Act, NZ SDS requirements
- Expiry alerting (Chemwatch doesn't do this particularly well)
- Simpler UX than enterprise platforms
- You can provide actual local support

### The Compliance Angle (Use This)
NZ schools have **legal obligations** under:
- Health and Safety at Work Act 2015
- Hazardous Substances and New Organisms Act 1996
- WorkSafe NZ guidelines for educational settings

A school without proper chemical management is **non-compliant and liable.** This is not a "nice to have" — it's a legal requirement. Frame your product around compliance, not just convenience.

### Recommended Pricing

**Model: One-off purchase + optional annual support/updates**

Schools often prefer one-off purchases for budget reasons (capital vs. operating budget can be different pools). But SaaS gives you recurring revenue. Consider a hybrid:

| Option | Price (NZD, ex GST) | Notes |
|--------|---------------------|-------|
| **Perpetual Licence** | $799 one-off | No annual commitment, updates for 1 year included |
| **Annual Subscription** | $349/yr | Ongoing updates, SDS library updates, email support |
| **Site Licence (multi-dept)** | $999/yr | All departments, priority support |

**Recommended default:** Push the **annual subscription** — frame it as "always current with NZ H&S requirements." Regulations change; they need to stay updated.

**Pricing rationale:**
- Cheaper than Chemwatch ($450–590) → easy to justify
- Significantly cheaper than ChemAlert → "enterprise value at school price"
- Enough margin to cover your time for support

### Go-to-Market

**This is your easiest first product to sell:**
- Science HoDs feel this pain every year at Chemwatch renewal time
- The renewal window is **February–March** (Chemwatch runs Mar–Feb)
- **Target outreach: November–January** before renewals hit

**First steps:**
1. Document your NZ school as a case study (before/after)
2. Email 5 Science HoDs you know personally in October/November
3. Offer a 3-month free trial to the first 5 schools
4. Ask for testimonials after trial

**Network to tap:**
- **NZASE (NZ Association of Science Educators)** — they have conferences and a newsletter; get a presentation slot
- **Science Learning Hub / Royal Society Te Apārangi** — good awareness channel
- Science department subject association groups (Facebook groups exist for NZ science teachers)

**One full-day school PLD booking = ~2 months of AI costs covered.**

### PLD Funding Reality in NZ

**MOE-funded PLD (the good news):**
- Schools receive a PLD budget they can spend with any provider
- Principals have discretion to spend this on AI-related PLD
- There is **no requirement** for you to be an "approved provider" for a school to pay you from their PLD budget
- Schools can also use **operational funds** for one-off PLD

**MOE-contracted PLD (the harder path):**
- Getting on the MOE's contracted provider list is a longer-term goal (12+ months away)
- 2026 MOE PLD priorities are: Maths, Literacy, Assessment — AI is NOT currently a funded priority
- But schools are buying AI PLD from their own budgets regardless

**How to get PLD bookings:**
1. Write a clear one-page "What I offer" document — principals forward these to each other
2. Offer your first school a discounted rate ($500) in exchange for a testimonial + referral
3. Present at your local **RTLB cluster meeting** or regional teacher network
4. Connect with **CORE Education** — they run PLD nationally and sometimes bring in associate consultants
5. Get on the **EdTechNZ** consultant directory

### Positioning Statement (Use This)
> "I help NZ schools implement AI tools practically — not just awareness, but actual workflows teachers use on Monday morning. As a working science teacher, I know what's realistic and what's hype."

## ⚖️ Legal & Business Setup Checklist

### Immediate (Do Now)
- [ ] **IP conversation with school** — see section above — this is #1 priority
- [ ] **Register sole trader** — free via business.govt.nz, takes 30 minutes
- [ ] **Get an NZBN** — automatic with registration, needed for invoicing
- [ ] **Open business bank account** — separate from personal, makes accounting clean

### Within 3 Months
- [ ] **Employment lawyer consult** — review your employment agreement for IP clauses (~$300)
- [ ] **Accounting software** — Wave (free) or Xero ($30/month) — worth it for tax time
- [ ] **AI subscription costs** — set these up as business expenses from day one
- [ ] **Privacy policy** — any tool handling school data (chemical inventories, student-adjacent info) needs one; use a template to start

### Sole Trader vs. Company — The Decision

**Start as sole trader if:**
- Revenue < $30,000/year in year 1
- Low liability risk (software tools for schools, workshops)
- You want simplicity

**Switch to company (Limited) when:**
- Revenue approaching $70,000/year (company tax 28% vs personal 33%+)
- You want liability protection (especially for software with compliance claims)
- You're contracting to larger organisations
- Registration: ~$165 + annual filing

### GST
- **Register if** you'll earn >$60,000 in any 12-month period
- **Voluntary registration** below $60,000 is possible and sometimes worth it (claim GST back on expenses including AI subscriptions)
- At your current AI spend ($659/month), voluntary GST registration could give you ~$100/month in GST refunds on expenses
